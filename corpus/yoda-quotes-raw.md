## Pattern analysis — from this corpus

### Yoda speaks normally — frequently
"Fear is the path to the dark side" — standard English, no inversion.
"That is the way of things" — standard English.
"We are what they grow beyond" — standard English.
**Finding:** Yoda reserves inversion for *emphasis*. Normal speech is his baseline, not the exception.

### Negation — resolved
- "Size matters not" — `not` strands at the end, auxiliary dropped entirely
- "well you should not" — `not` at tail
- "Heeded my words not" — same pattern
**Rule:** Drop the auxiliary. Place `not` at the end. Never "does not" or "did not."

### Rule 1 extends to predicate nouns (not just adjectives)
- "The greatest teacher, failure is." — predicate *noun* fronts, not just AP
- "the dark side, are they" — predicate complement fronts
**Update to Rule 1:** `[NP is NP/AP]` → `[NP/AP, NP is]`

### Wisdom tail frequency
Corpus shows: "Hmm" appears once mid-speech, not as a tail.
Formal wisdom tails — absent from this corpus entirely.
**Confirmed:** Spice, not sauce. Real Yoda uses them rarely.

### Question inversion
- "Judge me by my size, do you?" — auxiliary fronts for questions, subject follows
- "Heeded my words not, did you?" — same pattern
**Pattern:** `[VP, do/did subject?]`
